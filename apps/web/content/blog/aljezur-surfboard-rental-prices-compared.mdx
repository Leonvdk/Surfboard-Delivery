If you Google "surfboard rental Aljezur price" you get a wall of numbers that don't compare to each other. A €15/day soft-top from a Sagres hostel is not the same product as €25/day from a beach hut, is not the same as a delivered week package. Below is what the market actually looks like, how the pricing tricks work, and how to read a rate card so you don't overpay or underspec.

We run a rental business here, so bias flag: we published our prices publicly and don't require minimums. What follows is honest market context, not a competitor takedown.

## The three rental models in Costa Vicentina

**Walk-in surf-school rentals** — Arrifana Surf School, Aljezur Surf School, and similar. You show up at the beach hut, pick a board, they hand you a wetsuit if you need one. Typical daily rates €25–35 for a board, €10–15 for a wetsuit. **They don't do weekly rates.** They exist to serve students taking lessons and the walk-up "I want to try today" market. Great if you want one afternoon of surfing. Not designed for a week-long trip.

**Lodge-attached rentals** — Arrifana Surf Lodge, various hostels and surf houses. Publish daily *and* weekly rates. Board €20/day or €100/week. Wetsuit separate (€5/day or €25/week). You have to physically go to the lodge to pick up and drop off. Fine if you're staying there or nearby; annoying if your Airbnb is 8 km away.

**Delivery services** — us and one or two others. Board delivered to your accommodation, picked up at the end. Weekly or short-stay packages, wetsuit usually bundled or add-on. This is the model built for the modern Airbnb-based holiday.

## What the "€50/week" ads leave out

The lowest published weekly rates in this area belong to national delivery services quoting **€50/week board only**. Read the fine print: those rates require a **2-board minimum order**. A solo traveller renting one board isn't paying €50 — they're paying an "on request" rate that isn't published. So the number that lures the click isn't the number a solo person actually pays.

Similarly: "free delivery" from another operator has a **week-minimum rental** attached. If you only need boards for 3 or 4 days, delivery isn't free — you can't get delivery at all. You go pick up.

These aren't scams, they're perfectly reasonable business models. But they mean **the advertised weekly rate ≠ your bill** unless you match the operator's ideal order shape.

## Like-for-like: what does a 5-day rental with delivery to an Aljezur Airbnb actually cost?

Say you're two friends staying in Vale da Telha for 5 days, arriving on a Sunday, leaving Thursday. You want one board and one wetsuit each — a mid-level intermediate softtop and a 3/2 mm suit.

**Walk-in surf school**: Two boards × €30/day × 5 days = €300. Two wetsuits × €12 × 5 = €120. You drive to the beach hut every morning and afternoon. **Total: €420 plus fuel and time.**

**Lodge rental**: Two boards × €100/week × ceil(5/7) = you pay full weekly for 5 days. €200. Two wetsuits × €25 = €50. You drive to the lodge for pickup and dropoff. **Total: €250 plus fuel and time.**

**Delivery competitor with week minimum**: If the operator requires 7 nights, you can't book 5. If they take it, the delivered set weekly is around €110–140 for board+wetsuit × 2 = **€220–280**, delivered.

**Us**, at published prices: Full package (board + wetsuit) €140/person × 2 = **€280 delivered.** No minimum order, no minimum term, free delivery, and if the board doesn't fit you after day one we swap it free.

The numbers converge. What differs is **what has to go right for you to pay the lowest number** and **how much of your holiday you spend arranging gear** vs surfing.

## What isn't in the rate card

Three costs never show up as line items on any competitor's site:

- **Time.** A pickup-only rental costs 30–90 minutes of your day, both ways. In peak-season Aljezur where every restaurant needs a table booked and every parking spot is contested, 90 minutes is 60% of the actual usable surf window on a day with two tide changes.
- **Board mismatch.** If the beach hut hands you a 6'6 shortboard when you're a first-week intermediate on 4 ft summer mush, you're going to have a mediocre day. Level-mismatched rentals convert holiday time into frustration. This is why we ask your height, weight, and experience up front — and why we swap free if we get it wrong.
- **Payment friction.** Some operators want a card deposit that gets refunded weeks later, or want the full amount up front through a payment provider you've never heard of. Cash-on-arrival plus MB WAY / iDEAL / Wero later is friendlier for Dutch, German, Portuguese and French travellers than "please fill in your card details on this form."

## How to actually compare a rate card

1. **Convert everything to per-person day.** €120/week for board + wetsuit = €17/day. €280 for 5-day 2-board bundle = €28/person/day. Now you can compare.
2. **Ask whether delivery is included at your quantity.** "Free delivery" often means free above a threshold. Ask, don't assume.
3. **Ask about the daily rate for a stay shorter than a week.** Some operators price 3–6 days as if it's a full week; some prorate.
4. **Ask about swaps.** If the board doesn't work for you on day one, what happens? A yes on this is worth €10/day in avoided misery.
5. **Ask about payment.** Cash on arrival is fair, MB WAY / iDEAL / Wero / card via a secure link is fair. Anything that requires up-front payment through a service you can't recognise is a red flag.
