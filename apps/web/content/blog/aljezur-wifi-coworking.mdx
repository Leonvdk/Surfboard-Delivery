Aljezur is not Lisbon—and that is the point. Wifi is usually fine in modern stays, 4G is strong in town, and dedicated coworking is thin. Most remote workers surf early, work late morning to afternoon, and accept holiday-mode signal in some beach valleys.

## Wifi quality in Aljezur area

| Location type | What to expect |
| --- | --- |
| **Newer rentals / guesthouses** | Often **fibre or solid VDSL** — video calls usually OK |
| **Older rural houses** | **ADSL or slower** — fine for email; large uploads painful |
| **Coastal hamlets** | Variable — ask host for **speedtest screenshot** before booking |

**Before you book:** Message the host: **“What download/upload speeds do you typically see?”** If they only say **“wifi included”**, dig deeper for a work trip.

We see more **Starlink** setups in rural listings — great when it works; still **weather and obstructions** matter. Treat it as **bonus**, not guaranteed.

## Coworking spaces: the reality

**Dedicated coworking desks** in Aljezur town are **limited** compared to Ericeira or Lisbon. What exists can **change seasonally** — search maps the month you travel and read **recent reviews**.

Practical fallback most people use:

- **Quiet kitchen tables** in rentals with good fibre.
- **Hotel lobbies** in slightly larger Algarve towns if you are desperate — usually a **drive**.

Do not plan a **VC pitch week** assuming WeWork-grade redundancy unless you have verified speeds yourself.

## Cafés with workable wifi

Café wifi **rotates** with ownership and routers — names go out of date fast. Strategy instead of a stale list:

- Choose **cafés in Aljezur town** with **locals on laptops** mid-morning.
- Order **food or multiple drinks** if you camp for hours — small businesses survive on turnover.
- **Headphones** for calls; nobody wants your stand-up on speaker.

**Backup:** **personal hotspot** from a Portuguese SIM — often faster than overloaded guest wifi in August.

## Mobile data options

Major Portuguese carriers (**MEO**, **Vodafone**, **NOS**) cover **N120** and villages well; **dead patches** appear in **steep valleys** and some **beach car parks**.

| Tip | Why |
| --- | --- |
| **EU roaming** (if applicable) | Often enough for short trips |
| **Local prepaid SIM / eSIM** | Cheaper data for longer stays |
| **Download offline maps** | Navigation when signal dips |

## Calls and uploads

**Video calls:** Prefer **wired ethernet** if the host offers it — rare but gold.

**Large file uploads:** Schedule around **off-peak** hours or drive to **faster coffee** in **Lagos** if you must — **~40–45 minutes** each way, so only for emergencies.
