August on the Costa Vicentina means peak crowds and surf that is usually small and wind-affected until late-month swell hints. Plan dawn sessions, sheltered spots, and non-surf plans. If you accept that deal, August can still work—here is an honest guide to surfing Aljezur in August.

## Wave conditions

Most of August mirrors **July**: **NW** swell at its **weakest**, **Nortada** **strong** in the **afternoon**, **cleanest** conditions usually **before** the thermal wind builds.

**Late August** is worth watching: longer-period **NW** swells occasionally appear as systems shift—nothing guaranteed, but the month can **end** on a different note than it **began**. If you see a jump in period on the chart, treat it like a different season for a day or two: currents pick up, sets organize wider, and takeoff spots move compared with mushy high-summer norms.

**Tips:**

- **Early** or **sheltered**—same playbook as July; [Arrifana](/blog/arrifana-surf-guide) stays the obvious wind refuge (and the obvious crowd magnet).
- If peaks are packed, **etiquette** and **patience** matter—drop-ins ruin everyone’s day.
- For gear on weak waves: [what surfboard should I rent](/blog/what-surfboard-should-i-rent).

## Crowds and accommodation

August is peak season squared: car parks, rental demand, restaurant waits, and lineups all reflect it. Book lodging months ahead if you want proximity to the main breaks.

[Where to stay in Aljezur](/blog/where-to-stay-aljezur) compares bases; expect top-tier nightly prices in popular weeks.

August is also the month where “I will fix my pop-up on holiday” meets reality: small waves forgive a lot, but crowded peaks do not. If you are practicing basics, consider a lesson block early in the trip so you pick up local hazards and lineup flow before habits set. That is not upsell—it is fewer collisions and fewer wasted mornings.

**Tips:**

- **Weekday** sessions beat **Saturday** chaos.
- **Secondary** beaches offer **space** at the cost of **more** **wind** exposure—pick your trade-off.

## Beyond surfing

August is **festival** and **beach** energy—live music in villages, long evenings, swimming, and coastal walks at sunrise before the heat peaks. ## Portuguese holidays and beach logistics

August overlaps with Portuguese holiday weeks—expect more families, more bodyboards, and more swimmers inside the surf zone at popular beaches. It is not personal; it is summer. Set a wider berth, slow your takeoffs, and keep an eye on kids drifting into the line. Beach bars and ice cream queues grow long; carry snacks if you are surfing through lunch. Late August sometimes delivers a taste of autumn swell—if you see a longer period on the forecast, treat it with respect even if the air still feels like summer.
