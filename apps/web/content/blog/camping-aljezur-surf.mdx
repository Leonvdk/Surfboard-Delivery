Cheap nights near good waves are possible on the west coast with official campsites and van-friendly options—inside strict Costa Vicentina rules. Wild camping is not legally casual in Portugal; enforcement varies. Here is the straight version so you choose deliberately.

## Official camping near Aljezur (start here)

Your lowest-friction option is a **licensed campsite** with facilities. Names and management change — always verify opening dates, prices, and whether **surf vans** are welcome before you drive.

## Wild camping: the law vs what you see

**Legal default:** camping outside designated areas on **public land** is **not allowed** in the way many imagine "free camping" in Scandinavia.

**Reality:** you'll still see vans tucked on dirt tracks and tents above beaches. That doesn't make it **legal or risk-free** — **fines**, **night moves**, and **bad feeling with locals** happen, especially when toilet paper appears in the bushes.

**Our take:** if budget is tight, use **official sites** or **hostels**; if you're in a **self-contained van** with waste management, you're still subject to **municipal and park rules**. Don't build fires; don't leave trash; don't argue with rangers.

## Natural park enforcement (serious)

Much of the coastline around Aljezur sits in **protected park** status. That often means **stricter parking**, **no random overnight stays** in sensitive zones, and **higher scrutiny** in summer. The Vicentina is not the place to test "we'll probably be fine."

If vanlife draws you, read [vanlife Costa Vicentina](/blog/vanlife-costa-vicentina) alongside this post — same rules, more nuance.

## Van parking and "where everyone goes"

We're not publishing a map of stealth spots — they change, and the ethical line blurs fast. Safer approach:

- Pay for **legal overnight** where offered.
- Use **day car parks** for **day use only** — respect posted hours.
- Ask **locals or campsite staff** what's tolerated **this season** — enforcement shifts.

## What to expect on the ground

**Facilities:** hot showers on proper sites; **coin showers** sometimes; **laundry** worth doing mid-trip if you're in a wetsuit daily.

**Weather:** **summer** is easy; **spring/autumn** needs **warmer sleeping gear**; **winter** is quiet but **wet and windy** — match your tent to reality.

**Surf rhythm:** campers often surf **early** (quiet roads, morning glass) and **cook dinner** at the pitch — same flow as a villa, tighter quarters.

### FAQ

### Can I camp on the beach in Aljezur?

Assume **no** unless you're in a **designated camping area**. Sand dunes and foreshores are **especially sensitive**.

### Is Serrão open year-round?

**Often** with reduced services off-season — **confirm** before you plan January nights.

### Do campsites have surfboard storage?

**Varies** — message the site. **Soft-tops** fit more places than hard boards.

### What wetsuit do I need camping in April?

Usually **4/3mm** on the west coast; **5/3** if you run cold — aligns with our seasonal guidance on [/pricing](/pricing) and [wetsuit guide](/blog/wetsuit-guide-portugal).
