Surfing Aljezur properly almost always means a rental car. Beaches like Arrifana, Monte Clérigo, Amoreira, and Bordeira sit along tens of kilometres with no useful public transport between them. A car is how you match swell, tide, and wind day to day.

## Why a car matters for surfing here

| Without a car | With a car |
| --- | --- |
| Locked to one walkable beach | Pick the best spot for today’s forecast |
| Dependent on sparse local buses | Dawn patrol and sunset sessions on your schedule |
| Hard to carry food, spare wetsuit, recovery kit | Normal surf trip logistics |

Could you manage **without**? Only if you are **happy in one village** (often **Arrifana** or **Monte Clérigo**) and accept you might watch better conditions **10 minutes away** on your phone. For most visitors, that gets old by day three.

## Where to rent: Faro vs Lisbon airport

| Pickup | Drive to Aljezur | When to choose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Faro (FAO)** | ~1.5 h | Default — shortest drive, competitive rates |
| **Lisbon (LIS)** | ~3 h | Better flight deal, city break first, winter schedules |

**Porto** or **Seville** are occasional entry points — longer drives but valid if your routing already puts you there.

## What to look for in the vehicle

**Size:** Two people + luggage fit a **compact**. **Four adults + boards** needs planning — soft-tops do not slide into every boot.

**Roof carry:** Longboards and bigger funboards often ride **on the roof**. Our **Premium** package includes **roof rack pads** so you can strap safely (tell us when you book). Otherwise confirm whether the hire company allows roof straps or rent pads locally — gaffer tape and hope is not the move.

**Transmission:** **Manual** is cheaper and common in Portugal; book **automatic** early if you need it.

**Insurance:** Read what your **credit card** already covers. The desk will offer excess waivers — sometimes worth it, sometimes not. **Tyre/windscreen** cover is worth a thought on **dirt access roads** to some car parks.

## Tolls: Via Verde and the A22

The **A22** west of Faro uses **electronic tolls only** — **no cash lanes**. Options:

- **Via Verde transponder** from the rental company (small daily fee).
- **Register your plate** on the Via Verde visitors flow before driving.

Unpaid tolls become **admin fees** from the hire firm. Not worth skipping.

## Driving in Portugal: quick tips

- **Right-hand traffic**; **roundabouts** everywhere — yield to traffic **on the roundabout**.
- **Speed cameras** on motorways and some N-roads.
- **N120** north of Lagos is scenic — **expect tractors**, **cyclists**, and **tourist vans**.
- **Mobile signal** drops on some valley roads — download **offline maps**.

## Parking at Aljezur surf beaches

| Beach | Parking notes |
| --- | --- |
| **Arrifana** | Main car park; **paid** in peak summer sometimes |
| **Monte Clérigo** | Large dirt/gravel area; **paid** possible high season |
| **Amoreira** | Smaller car park; arrive early summer weekends |
| **Vale Figueiras** | Dirt track and limited spaces — **early** wins |

**Free** is normal **outside** July–August peaks. **Theft from cars** is not rampant but **hide valuables** — same as any surf town worldwide.

## Fuel and supplies

**Fill in Aljezur town** on the **N120** — you do not want to detour to **Lagos** mid-week for diesel. Stock **water and snacks** before evening sessions; village shops close earlier than city habits.

## FAQ

### Do I need a 4x4 for Aljezur surf beaches?
**No** for normal access — a **standard car** handles maintained roads. Some **last metres** are bumpy dirt — drive slowly.

### Can I use Uber between beaches?
**Unreliable** — limited drivers, patchy signal, surge pricing in summer. Not a strategy for dawn patrol.

### Should I add a roof box?
Usually **no** for surf — boxes conflict with boards. **Fold rear seats** or **roof pads** instead.

### Is parking safe overnight at accommodation?
Generally **yes** in villages; still **do not leave** laptops on the seat.
