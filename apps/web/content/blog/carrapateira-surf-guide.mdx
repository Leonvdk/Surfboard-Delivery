Carrapateira is a small village perched on a hill between two of the best surf beaches on the Costa Vicentina: Bordeira to the north and Amado to the south. It's about 25–30 minutes by car from Aljezur and has a quiet, unhurried feel that attracts surfers, hikers, and people who like their villages without traffic lights. If you're basing yourself in the Aljezur area, Carrapateira is well worth a day trip — or more.

## The two beaches

Carrapateira's appeal is that you get two very different surf experiences within five minutes of each other.

### Bordeira (Praia da Bordeira)

A massive, open beach break to the north of the village. Wide, powerful, and cut through by the Ribeira da Carrapateira river. The waves are strong and the sandbanks shift constantly, making each session different from the last. Best for intermediate to advanced surfers who can read a lineup and handle currents. On a good day with offshore wind, the waves here are excellent. [Read our full Bordeira guide](/blog/bordeira-surf-guide).

### Amado (Praia do Amado)

A shorter, more contained beach to the south. Amado is one of the most popular surf beaches in the Algarve — several surf schools operate here year-round, and the waves tend to be more consistent and better-shaped than Bordeira. The southern end picks up less swell and is where the schools teach beginners. The northern end and outside section offer steeper, faster waves for intermediates. The beach has a restaurant, showers, and a car park right at the sand.

**Choosing between them:** On any given day, one usually works better than the other. Bordeira catches more swell and tends to be better on smaller days. Amado handles bigger swells more gracefully and stays cleaner in light cross-shore winds. Check both from their viewpoints before deciding — it takes five minutes to drive between them.

## Conditions

### Swell

Both beaches face west and catch plenty of swell. Bordeira is more exposed and picks up everything; Amado is slightly more sheltered by the headland to its south and filters the swell a little.

- **0.5–1m W/WNW swell:** Amado will have small but surfable waves. Bordeira might be too soft.
- **1–1.5m W/WNW swell:** Both beaches work well. This is the sweet spot.
- **2m+ swell:** Bordeira gets heavy. Amado stays more organised but the outside section gets powerful. Beginners should stay on the inside at Amado or sit this one out.

### Tide

Both beaches work through most of the tide. Mid tide is the safest bet at Bordeira, where low tide makes the beach impossibly wide and high tide steepens the shorebreak. Amado is more forgiving across the tidal range — it produces surfable waves from low through high, though mid tide on the push is still the best window.

### Wind

Offshore is east or northeast at both beaches. Neither has meaningful wind shelter — they're open, west-facing beaches. Morning sessions are consistently cleaner. In summer, expect the onshore sea breeze from midday onwards. Autumn and winter bring more frequent offshore days that can last all day.

## Seasonal breakdown

**Summer (June–September):** The best season for beginners and intermediates at Amado. Smaller, warmer swells (19–21°C water, [3/2mm wetsuit](/surf-gear)) and lighter winds. Amado gets busy with surf schools and day trippers. Bordeira stays quiet. Morning sessions at either beach are the pick — by afternoon, the sea breeze usually makes things choppy.

**Autumn (October–November):** Swells build, crowds vanish, and both beaches come alive. This is the best season for experienced surfers. Bordeira can produce powerful, well-shaped peaks. Amado becomes a more serious wave with faster sections. Water cools to 17–18°C and a 4/3mm wetsuit is more comfortable. October in particular is outstanding.

**Winter (December–February):** Big Atlantic storms drive heavy swell onto both beaches. Bordeira can be too powerful and messy on the biggest days. Amado holds its shape better but still demands respect. For advanced surfers, the uncrowded winter lineups here are rewarding. Water drops to 14–16°C — you'll want a [5/3mm wetsuit with boots](/surf-gear).

**Spring (March–May):** Transitional and varied. Late spring often brings clean, moderate swells that work beautifully at both beaches. The water warms slowly (16–18°C). Fewer visitors than summer, better waves than you'd expect. A 4/3mm wetsuit covers most spring sessions.

## The village

Carrapateira is small — a few hundred residents, a church, and a cluster of houses along narrow streets. But it has what you need:

- **Restaurants:** A handful of good options. Sítio do Forno is popular for grilled meats and fish. There's also a pizzeria and a couple of cafés.
- **Shops:** One small grocery for basics. For a proper shop, you'll need Aljezur town.
- **Accommodation:** Some guesthouses and rental apartments in the village itself. The vibe is quieter and more rural than staying in Aljezur or Arrifana.
- **Rota Vicentina:** The village is on the Fisherman's Trail, one of Portugal's best long-distance hiking routes. The coastal walk from Carrapateira to Arrifana is spectacular.

## Carrapateira vs Aljezur area

If you're deciding where to base yourself — around Aljezur or around Carrapateira — here's what to consider:

| | Aljezur area | Carrapateira |
|---|---|---|
| **Main beaches** | Arrifana, Monte Clérigo, Amoreira, Vale Figueiras | Bordeira, Amado |
| **Best for beginners** | Monte Clérigo, Arrifana centre | Amado |
| **Best for advanced** | Vale Figueiras, Arrifana point, Amoreira | Bordeira |
| **Town facilities** | Full town (Aljezur): supermarkets, restaurants, market | Small village: a few restaurants, one shop |
| **Accommodation range** | Wide — from hostels to villas | Limited — guesthouses and apartments |
| **Distance between** | 25–30 minutes by car | 25–30 minutes by car |

Most visiting surfers base themselves near Aljezur and drive to Carrapateira when conditions suit. The extra 30-minute drive is easy and gives you access to both areas' beaches. [Our accommodation guide](/blog/where-to-stay-aljezur) covers the main options around Aljezur.

## Getting there from Aljezur

Drive south on the N268 towards Vila do Bispo. After about 20 km, turn right following signs to Carrapateira. The road is fully paved and straightforward. From Aljezur town it takes about 25–30 minutes. From Arrifana or Vale da Telha, add 5–10 minutes.

No public transport runs between Aljezur and Carrapateira, so you'll need a car.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Carrapateira worth visiting for a day?

Absolutely. Drive down in the morning, check both Bordeira and Amado from their viewpoints, surf the better one, then have lunch in the village. It's an easy day trip from anywhere in the [Aljezur area](/blog/complete-guide-surfing-aljezur) and gives you access to two excellent beaches you won't find closer to town.

### Which beach is better for beginners — Amado or Bordeira?

Amado, without question. Multiple surf schools operate there because the waves are consistent, well-shaped, and manageable for learners. Bordeira is more powerful and unpredictable. If you're [surfing for the first time](/blog/first-time-surfing-portugal), Amado is the choice in the Carrapateira area.

### Can I rent boards at Carrapateira?

The surf schools at Amado offer lesson-and-board packages, but standalone board rental options at the beach are limited. We deliver boards and wetsuits to accommodations across the Aljezur area — if you're staying within reasonable distance of Carrapateira, [get in touch](/contact) and we'll work something out.

### How does Amado compare to Arrifana?

Both are good all-round beaches. Arrifana has more variety (the right-side point break is something Amado can't match) and better wind shelter thanks to the cliffs. Amado has more consistent shape across the tidal range and is slightly easier for beginners. If you have a [week in the area](/blog/one-week-aljezur-itinerary), surf both.

### Is Carrapateira a good base for a surf trip?

It can work, especially if you prefer quiet village life over the relative bustle of Aljezur town. The trade-off is fewer restaurants, shops, and accommodation options. Most surfers find it easier to [stay near Aljezur](/blog/where-to-stay-aljezur) and drive to Carrapateira when the beaches there look good.
