## Wave conditions over Easter week

Easter does not create its own swell—Atlantic systems do. You might get fun shoulder-high weeks, flat spells, or a punchy pulse if a storm tracks favorably. Compared with summer, there is usually more power on tap; compared with January, less day-to-day consistency.

Wind is generally kinder than peak Nortada months, but afternoon breezes still appear. Morning sessions are the default plan.

**Tips:**

- Check forecasts daily—spring swaps personalities fast.
- Beginners: pick mellow sand peaks on smaller days; do not assume every day is soft.

## Easter traditions and town life in Aljezur

Portugal’s Easter week (Semana Santa) blends religious processions, family meals, and time off work. In smaller towns you may see processions or services—respectful distance and quiet cameras go a long way. Aljezur’s historic centre—Moorish quarter and modern town—feels especially alive when locals are home for the holiday.

Practical effect: some businesses trim hours on Good Friday or Easter Sunday; others stay open for visitors. When in doubt, ask.

## Accommodation: book before the rush

Easter week fills guesthouses and rentals fast—especially places near [Arrifana](/blog/arrifana-surf-guide) or Monte Clérigo. Prices often jump for the holiday window even though it is not July.

## Family-friendly angle

Sand beaches, shallow sections on small days, and hiking trails make the area work for mixed groups—surfers and non-surfers alike. Bring warm layers for kids after sessions; spring wind cuts when you are wet.

## Travel practicalities (flights, hire cars, and boards)

Easter week can mean busier airports and higher car-hire prices across Portugal—not unique to Aljezur, but worth budgeting. If you are bringing your own boards, book roof straps and check airline fees early; if you are renting locally, reserve gear for the holiday window because demand spikes. Traffic on the coastal road moves slower when everyone is heading to the same beaches—build buffer time, especially on Good Friday and Easter Monday when day-trip patterns shift.

## Why Easter might be the right week for you

Pick Easter if you like shoulder-season waves, longer days, and a bit of holiday atmosphere—and you are fine booking early. Skip it if you want the cheapest quiet week or guaranteed flat-spell avoidance—no calendar fixes the Atlantic.
