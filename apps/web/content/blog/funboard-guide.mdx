A funboard—mini-mal or mid-length—usually sits near 7'0–8'0 and handles most holiday conditions without feeling like a tank or a toothpick. At Surf Rental Aljezur we stock 7'0 and 7'8 funboards among other sizes; here is what that shape actually does in the water.

## What makes a board a funboard

**Length** in the **low- to mid-seven foot** range for many rental fleets.

**Volume** higher than a performance shortboard, lower than a classic longboard — enough **paddle** without full **8'6** swing weight.

**Outline** — often a **rounded nose**, **moderate rocker**, **fairly wide** midsection. Designed to **catch waves** and **turn** better than a log, more stable than a shortboard.

Not every brand uses the word “funboard” on the deck pad — it is a **category**, not a trademark.

## Why funboards are the best all-round choice for many travellers

**Aljezur’s beaches** ([Monte Clérigo](/blog/monte-clerigo-surf-guide), [Arrifana](/blog/arrifana-surf-guide), [Amado](/blog/amado-surf-guide)) shift with **sandbars** and **swell direction**. A week might include **knee-high** runners and **head-high** walls — not XXL, but variable.

A funboard **bridges** those days better than extremes:

- More **maneuverable** than an **8'6** for tighter beach-break peaks
- More **forgiving** than a **6'6** if your paddling or timing is still inconsistent

If you are past **absolute day one** but not a confident shortboarder, the fun zone is often where **progress feels fastest**. [First time surfing Portugal](/blog/first-time-surfing-portugal) mindset still applies: **position**, **patience**, **safety**.

**Heavy adults** or **very tall** beginners may still prefer **8'6** — volume rules. See [what surfboard should I rent](/blog/what-surfboard-should-i-rent) for the full decision tree.

## Limitations (honest)

**Very small, weak swell** — a **longboard** still wins wave count.

**Steep, fast, hollow faces** — a **skilled** shortboarder on appropriate hard equipment has an edge; a **funboard** can still work on **many** Costa Vicentina days, but it is not a **Pipe** tool.

**Duck-diving** — thick foam funboards are **hard** to sink; **turtle roll** and **smart channel choice** matter. [How to duck dive](/blog/how-to-duck-dive) explains why.

## Session pacing on a funboard

Because funboards bridge sizes, improvers sometimes over-surf — they catch more waves per hour and tire faster than on a day spent fighting a shortboard. Two shorter sessions with food and hydration between often beat one marathon that ends in sloppy technique and sore shoulders. Pair that habit with [post surf recovery](/blog/post-surf-recovery) and you will still be smiling on day six of the trip.

### Funboard vs fish?

“Fish” usually means a **wide, short** twin or quad — different design goals. Funboard here means **mid-length** **7–8ft** **all-rounder**.

### Can I learn from zero on a 7'0?

Possible for **athletic** learners; **7'8** or **8'6** is **kinder** for most **first week** surfers.

### Is a funboard “slow”?

It is **faster to paddle** than a **6'6** short shape for most people — “slow” is usually **technique**, not the board category.

### One board for a two-week trip?

**7'8** is the **classic** answer for **mixed** conditions and **improving** riders — adjust if you are **very** light/heavy per our sizing post.
