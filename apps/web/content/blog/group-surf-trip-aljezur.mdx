A week with mates on the west coast is hard to beat — if you sort accommodation, cars, and surf expectations before you arrive. Aljezur scales well for groups: big villas, several beaches within a short drive, and enough restaurant capacity in town if you don't all cook every night. Here's a straight-talking playbook for four to eight people.

## Why villas beat hotels for surf crews

You'll want **board storage**, a kitchen, outdoor space for wetsuits, and enough bathrooms that nobody mutinies. The Aljezur area is full of multi-bedroom houses in Vale da Telha, near Arrifana, and around Monte Clérigo. Book one with a **hose or outdoor shower** if you can — sandy footpaths and rental gear don't mix well with white sofas.

| Group size | Cars | Accommodation tip |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 4 | 1 compact + boards on roof OR 2 cars | 2-bedroom+ with parking |
| 6 | 2 cars ideal | 3-bedroom villa, check driveway width |
| 8 | 2–3 cars | 4-bedroom+; confirm parking for all |

**Tip:** Satellite maps lie less than listing photos — check access roads, especially down to Arrifana.

## Mixed abilities: split smart, meet for lunch

Not everyone should surf the same peak at the same time.

- **Beginners / returners:** Monte Clérigo, mild days at Arrifana (inside), or structured lessons.
- **Intermediates:** Arrifana main peak, Amoreira on the right tide.
- **Advanced:** Same beaches on bigger days — but respect [safety](/blog/surf-safety-portugal) and lifeguard flags.

Agree on **a WhatsApp group pin** for the lunch spot — "O Sargo at 1pm" beats eight people wandering with pruny fingers.

## Daily rhythm that keeps the group happy

**Early:** Optional dawn patrol crew goes to Arrifana or the call of the forecast.

**Mid-morning:** Second session or beach hang / hike on the Rota Vicentina.

**Lunch:** One cooked meal at the villa OR a beach bar / town restaurant.

**Afternoon:** Surf again or siesta — August heat makes naps strategic.

**Evening:** BBQ at home (cheapest, best for eight) or book a table in Aljezur town. See [best restaurants](/blog/best-restaurants-aljezur) for starters.

## Evenings beyond the villa

Pub quizzes and mega-clubs aren't the draw. Expect **cards, playlists, sunset drives, and maybe one night out** in town for drinks. If someone needs a bigger night, Lagos is ~45 minutes — nominate drivers upfront.

### FAQ

### How many boards should a group of six rent?

Usually **one board per surfing adult**, sometimes **one shared learner board** if two beginners alternate in the water. Tell us your headcount and levels and we'll suggest a quiver.

### Can we mix wetsuit thicknesses in one group?

Yes — spring and autumn often mean **4/3 for some and 3/2 for others** depending on cold tolerance. We'll size everyone individually.

### Is Arrifana too crowded for a big group?

Summer peaks get busy — split into **two take-off zones** or stagger sessions. Monte Clérigo spreads people out more on small-to-mid days.

### What if part of the group doesn't surf?

Send them hiking, to the Saturday market, or on a Sagres day trip — [non-surf activities](/blog/non-surf-activities-aljezur) covers ideas.
