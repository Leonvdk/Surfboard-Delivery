## Wave conditions

**Swell**: July averages the **least powerful** surf of the year along this coast. When a pulse arrives, it is often **small and fun**—great for learning, repetitive takeoffs, and longboarding. **Flat** stretches happen; check forecasts daily and stay flexible on which beach you drive to.

**Nortada**: typically **strongest June–August**, often **afternoon**. Exposed west-facing sand can turn **onshore and choppy** by late morning. **Morning** is your premium window—**glassier**, **lighter wind**, fewer people in the water.

**Tips:**

- Treat **6:30–9:00** as prime time if the forecast shows afternoon wind.
- [Arrifana](/blog/arrifana-surf-guide) draws crowds for a reason—**cliff shelter** from north wind—but arrive **early**; the car park tells the story by mid-morning.
- On tiny days, **volume** wins—[what surfboard should I rent](/blog/what-surfboard-should-i-rent) explains why.

## What to wear

**~19°C** water: **3/2mm** wetsuit is standard. Sun protection on skin and lips matters—July sun is serious. Hydrate; heat and neoprene plus session length can sneak up on you.

## Crowds and accommodation

July is the **busiest** month: international visitors, Portuguese families on holiday, and every popular beach at **capacity** by midday on good-weather weekends.

**Book accommodation far ahead**—especially walking distance to Arrifana or Monte Clérigo. Last-minute deals are rare in peak weeks. [Where to stay in Aljezur](/blog/where-to-stay-aljezur) helps you pick a base that matches how you want to move.

**Pricing** hits **annual highs**; expect to pay peak rates for guesthouses, villas, and apartments.

One more July truth: the ocean can feel busy even when the waves are small, because everyone is looking at the same forecast apps and the same two beaches. If you have a car and patience, rotating between a sheltered peak and a more exposed beach—depending on wind—keeps the week fresher than repeating the same frustrated dawn patrol at the same packed takeoff.

## Beyond surfing

July is made for **early surf**, **long lunch**, **siesta**, and **late swim** when the wind eases. Explore side beaches, the river at Amoreira, or sunset viewpoints when the lineup is blown out.

## Heat, hydration, and respectful lineups

July heat is real—neoprene plus sun plus a midday beach walk can wipe you out before your second session. Carry water, seek shade between surfs, and do not underestimate UV bouncing off the sand. In the water, crowded peaks mean clear communication: call your direction early, do not snake, and paddle wide on the way back out. A bad summer session is rarely about wave quality alone; it is about friction with other people. If the main peak feels tense, walk five minutes—space often fixes mood faster than a new board.
