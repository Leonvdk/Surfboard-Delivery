Mareta is Sagres' town beach: south-facing sand at Europe's south-west tip, about forty minutes from Aljezur. When northwest wind trashes exposed west beaches, Mareta's angle can stay cleaner; on small swell it is often friendlier than raw Cordoama—still ocean, still currents.

It is not a magic flat-water pool: pick your day, surf mornings in summer, and stay realistic about size.

## The layout and how it breaks

Mareta is a crescent of sand backed by cliffs and town. Waves reform on the inside on small days, which suits practice — pop-ups, trim, turning.

| Factor | Typical effect |
|--------|----------------|
| Swell size | Small = learner playground; bigger = fast walls and busier peaks |
| Swell direction | South swells can aim into this bay — check alongside [Tonel](/blog/tonel-surf-guide) |
| Wind | N/NW often more manageable here than on west beaches |
| Tide | Moves shorebreak and inside shape — quick visual check before paddling |

## Beginner-friendly — with caveats

**Yes, when:** knee- to waist-high waves, light wind, and you already have basics from a lesson or [first-time surfing Portugal](/blog/first-time-surfing-portugal) prep.

**No, when:** overhead sets, strong rips, or a dense holiday pack — the beach is popular; etiquette and awareness matter.

Compared to [Arrifana centre](/blog/arrifana-surf-guide), Mareta is farther from an Aljezur base, but easier than [Beliche](/blog/beliche-surf-guide) stairs for families juggling gear and kids.

## Facilities and town convenience

Cafés, restaurants, and shops sit minutes away — a luxury after dirt-road beaches. Parking in peak season can frustrate; circle calmly or park slightly farther and walk.

Showers and toilets vary by season and business hours — plan like an adult and carry water for a rinse if needed.

## Who else is in the water

Holiday surfers, locals, bodyboarders (we do not rent bodyboards — bring your own if that is your thing), and sometimes lessons. Communicate, give way, and do not snake inside reforms.

Refresh manners on our [surf etiquette](/blog/surf-etiquette) page if needed.

## Pair Mareta with other Sagres checks

On a south-coast day, surf checks often run Mareta → Tonel → Beliche — order depends on the forecast. Our [Tonel surf guide](/blog/tonel-surf-guide) explains the reef side; [Beliche](/blog/beliche-surf-guide) covers the cove when west energy is big.

## Safety

- Rips still exist — especially on bigger days when the inside looks harmless from the cliff.
- Shorebreak can pitch at certain tides — protect your neck and your board; bail flat when in doubt.
- Cold water outside high summer — match wetsuit thickness to the month ([wetsuit guide Portugal](/blog/wetsuit-guide-portugal)).

## Day trip from an Aljezur base

If you are staying in Aljezur for a week, Sagres is realistic as a half-day or full-day mission: leave early, surf Mareta while the wind is light, then check whether [Tonel](/blog/tonel-surf-guide) or [Beliche](/blog/beliche-surf-guide) offers a better second session. Pack food — restaurant queues in August eat surf time. Fuel up in Vila do Bispo if you want to avoid Sagres petrol premiums on busy weekends.

That rhythm works well when the west coast is blown or huge and you still want salt water. Compare that strategy with our [Aljezur vs Sagres surf](/blog/aljezur-vs-sagres-surf) article if you are deciding where to book accommodation for the whole trip rather than just one chase day.
