## Wave conditions around New Year

You are in the heart of the North Atlantic’s active season. NW swell is more consistent and powerful than in summer, with longer intervals and heavier sets when charts light up. Storm systems can deliver excellent waves—or dangerous conditions for the wrong spot and skill level.

**Tips:**

- Read forecasts and buoy data; winter rewards patience and punishes guessing.
- Match break to level; when in doubt, watch a full set cycle before paddling out.

## NYE in Aljezur vs Lagos

Aljezur on 31 December tends to be low-key—cafés, restaurants, friends, maybe a small event depending on the year. If you want a bigger party, fireworks, and late venues, Lagos (roughly **~45 minutes** south by car, traffic depending) is the usual answer for visitors who want that energy. You can sleep in Aljezur, surf in the morning, drive down for the evening, and slip back north when the roads quiet.

Always use a designated driver or a taxi—rural roads and celebration do not mix.

## What to wear in the water

**~15°C** water and winter air: **5/3mm** is standard. Boots protect feet on reef and keep sessions comfortable; gloves are optional unless you feel the cold fast. After surfing, dry robes and warm layers matter—wind on wet skin bites.

## Accommodation and winter rates

January-adjacent weeks often bring better value than July—more availability, lower nightly prices in many guesthouses and villas. Christmas can tighten some dates right before NYE, so book early if you want a specific house or sea view.

## A sensible New Year’s surf day (short daylight)

Winter rewards a simple rhythm: check the forecast the night before, aim to be parking near your chosen break not long after sunrise, and surf the best window before wind or tide turns the lineup messy. With only about nine and a half to ten hours of daylight, chasing three beaches in one day usually backfires—pick one primary spot, keep a backup in mind, and build in time to warm up between sessions. If a big storm arrives, default to a walk on the cliffs, a slow lunch, and a honest reassessment tomorrow.
