Booking a solo Friday-to-Monday in an Aljezur Airbnb is more common every year. Portuguese long weekends, the Faro–Aljezur car rental workflow, the Lisbon-based digital nomad crowd running down for a break — you're not alone. But you *are* alone in the specific sense that most surf-rental services in this area price and staff themselves for **families and 2+ groups on week-long trips.** Advertised free delivery often disappears when you're just one person on 3 days. This is a guide to the small-order market and how to actually get a board dropped at your door for a short solo stay.

## The small-order problem

Delivery is not free labour. If a business has a van, a driver, a stock of boards, and an accommodation address, the economics of "drive out to Vale da Telha to hand over one board" have to balance somehow. Every operator solves this differently:

- **Minimum term** (e.g. "free delivery on 7+ day rentals only"). If you booked 3 days, delivery isn't free — sometimes isn't offered at all.
- **Minimum quantity** (e.g. "€50/week per board, minimum 2 boards"). Great for a couple, not helpful for a solo traveller.
- **Delivery fee** (a flat €10–30 charged separately). Fine, but if you're only spending €54 on a 3-day rental, adding €25 delivery each way means you pay more for logistics than gear.
- **Pickup-only for small orders** (i.e. you drive to their shop). Kills the point of a delivery service.

None of these are unreasonable business decisions. But they all mean the same thing: **the "free delivery" advertised on the homepage might not apply to you.**

## What weekend Aljezur is actually like

Off-season Fri–Sun trips (October–May, excluding Christmas) get you the best of Aljezur: **empty lineups**, mid-sized swells at Arrifana, Bordeira firing on the right combination, and enough restaurants open in Aljezur town to have a fine dinner without booking. Peak-season (July–August) weekends are busier — Amado especially fills up with schools — but Aljezur is still a quieter option than Ericeira or Peniche on the same dates.

Two forecasts matter for a short trip: **swell and wind**. A weekend with even 1.5 m of swell at 12+ seconds and any offshore wind angle gives you real waves on at least one day. A flat weekend with onshore wind gives you nothing — but at least you'll have swum and eaten well. Check magicseaweed / Surfline / Windguru a few days before you fly. If it looks flat, we're happy to reschedule at no cost as long as you tell us within 72 hours of booking.

## Airbnb specifics

We deliver to any accommodation address in the four covered towns. In practice that means:

- **Aljezur town** — the old town, and the newer district by the market. Any short-term rental within the town boundary.
- **Arrifana** — the cliff-top houses and apartments overlooking the beach.
- **Vale da Telha** — the villa estate south of Aljezur. This is where most family bookings land; also common for solo travellers because the villas are affordable off-season.
- **Monte Clérigo** — the little beach village north of Arrifana.

If your Airbnb is in a slightly different town (Rogil, Odeceixe, Carrapateira) message us — we frequently extend delivery case-by-case, especially for a short stay when we're already on the road for other drops.
