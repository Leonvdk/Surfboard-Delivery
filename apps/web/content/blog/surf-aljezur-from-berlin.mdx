Berlin surfers are showing up more in Aljezur every season — cheap flights to the Algarve, reliable Atlantic swell, and none of the North Sea chill. From Berlin you usually fly **Faro** or **Lisbon**, pick up a car, and drive **west to the Costa Vicentina**. Here is how to plan it without surprises.

## Flights: Berlin to Faro

**Ryanair** operates **Berlin → Faro** on many dates in season; flight time is roughly **3.5 hours**. Schedules tighten in deep winter — always check **BER** (and sometimes legacy search still shows SXF where relevant) for your exact week.

| Route | When it makes sense |
| --- | --- |
| BER → FAO direct | Shortest overall journey when available |
| BER → LIS direct | Often more frequency year-round; longer drive after |
| BER → FAO via another EU hub | Only if direct prices are extreme |

**Lisbon** is a solid alternative: multiple carriers, then **~3 hours’ drive** south-west to Aljezur. If you find a cheap fare to Lisbon and expensive Faro, do the maths with **fuel + tolls + time**.

## Flights: Berlin to Lisbon

**easyJet**, **Ryanair**, **TAP**, and others connect **Berlin Brandenburg (BER)** to **Lisbon** regularly. Treat **LIS** as your hub if:

- You want a **city break** before the coast.
- **Winter** Faro frequencies drop and Lisbon stays cheaper.
- You are combining Portugal with **Ericeira or Peniche** later (longer trip).

## Car rental and driving

Pick up at **Faro** or **Lisbon** airport. Non-negotiables:

- **Via Verde** or confirmed toll solution for **A22** (Faro side).
- **Early booking** for July–August and German school holidays.
- **Full-to-full** fuel.

**Faro → Aljezur:** ~**1.5 hours** via **A22** then **N120**.  
**Lisbon → Aljezur:** ~**3 hours** via **A2** then **N120** west.

German visitors sometimes underestimate how **spread out** the surf beaches are — Arrifana, Monte Clérigo, Amoreira, and Vale Figueiras are not a single “spot.” [Car rental for Aljezur surf](/blog/car-rental-aljezur-surf) explains why keys matter as much as plane tickets.

## Berlin-specific tips: holidays, noise, and combo trips

**School holidays:** When **German states** break for summer or autumn, **Faro** flights and **Algarve** cars jump in price together — booking **both** early matters more than chasing the last €20 on the fare alone.

**Noise and comfort:** Early **BER** departures beat **midday heat** in July — you land with time to **shop in Lagos or Aljezur** before dark. If Ryanair’s Faro schedule is thin on your week, **Lisbon** plus the **three-hour drive** is a boring but reliable fallback.

**Combo ideas:** Some Berlin crews pair **Aljezur** with **Lisbon** (city first) or tack on **Sagres** (40 minutes south of Aljezur when traffic behaves) for variety. You are already paying for the car — use it. [Complete guide surfing Aljezur](/blog/complete-guide-surfing-aljezur) explains how the west coast differs from the **south Algarve** strip.

## Surf scene and when to go

**April–May** and **September–October** balance **crowds, water temp, and swell**; **June–August** is busiest and flattest some years but still fun on a mal. [Best time to surf Aljezur](/blog/best-time-to-surf-aljezur) goes month by month without selling you a fantasy.

## FAQ

### How long is Berlin to Faro by plane?
About **3 to 3.5 hours** on direct flights, depending on aircraft and routing.

### Is one week enough?
Yes — [one week Aljezur itinerary](/blog/one-week-aljezur-itinerary) is a good template. Two weeks lets you rest more and explore south toward Sagres.

### Do Germans need an IDP in Portugal?
EU licences are fine. For some non-EU licences an **International Driving Permit** may be required — verify for your licence type before travel.
