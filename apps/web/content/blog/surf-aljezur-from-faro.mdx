Most people who surf Aljezur fly into **Faro**. It is the nearest major airport — about **1.5 hours’ drive** to Aljezur on a good day — and car rental competition keeps prices reasonable. There is no direct public bus to Aljezur; you either drive or connect through Lagos. Here is the practical breakdown.

## How far is Aljezur from Faro Airport?

| From | Drive time (typical) | Main roads |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Faro Airport (FAO) | ~1 h 30 min | A22 west, exit towards Aljezur, N120 north |
| Faro city centre | +10–15 min | Same route |

The **A22 (Via do Infante)** is fast and quiet; tolls are **electronic only** — no booths. Use a **Via Verde transponder** from the rental desk or register your plate on the Via Verde visitors flow before you drive. The **N120** north of Lagos winds through cork forest; take it easy on bends and occasional slow traffic.

## Driving route from Faro to Aljezur

1. Leave the airport and follow signs to the **A22 west** (towards **Portimão / Lagos**).
2. Stay on the A22 until the exit for **Aljezur** / **N120** (junction numbering varies — GPS is reliable).
3. **N120 north** to Aljezur town, then branch toward **Arrifana**, **Monte Clérigo**, or **Vale da Telha** depending on your stay.

**Tolls:** Budget roughly **€5–10** each way on the A22 segment you use; exact amounts depend on entry/exit points.

**Parking at beaches:** Mostly free outside peak summer; July–August can bring **paid parking** at busy spots like Arrifana and Monte Clérigo. Early arrival helps.

## Car rental at Faro Airport

This is the default move for surfers. Tips we give friends:

- **Book ahead** — especially June–September; last-minute prices jump.
- **Compact vs estate** — two people + bags fit a small car; longboards rarely fit inside a hatchback — we supply **roof rack pads on Premium** so you can strap a board safely (message us when you book).

## Flights to Faro

Faro is served year-round with spikes in spring and summer. Typical carriers from the UK and Europe include **Ryanair**, **easyJet**, **Jet2**, **Transavia**, **Wizz Air**, and others depending on season. Schedules thin out in midwinter but rarely disappear entirely.

If your dates are flexible, **midweek arrivals** often beat Friday–Sunday surcharges.

## No direct bus: Lagos transfer

There is **no coach straight from Faro airport to Aljezur**. Realistic public route:

- Bus or train **Faro → Lagos** (roughly **1.5–2+ hours** depending on service).
- **Local bus Lagos → Aljezur** — **limited timetable**; check the same day’s departures or you wait hours.

Total time can exceed **4 hours** with connections. Fine for budget travellers who will stay in one village; poor for a surf trip where you want to check **Arrifana**, **Amoreira**, and **Vale Figueiras** in one week. ## FAQ

### How long from Faro Airport to Aljezur by car?
About **1 hour 30 minutes** in normal traffic, mostly motorway then the N120.

### Do I need a Via Verde transponder for the A22?
Strongly recommended. The A22 has no cash lanes; without a transponder or registered plate, you risk admin fees and fines later.

### Is there Uber from Faro to Aljezur?
Sometimes available; expensive for a 90-minute trip and you still need transport between beaches all week. A rental car is almost always better value for surfers.
