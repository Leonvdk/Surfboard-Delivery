Paris to Aljezur is a classic long-weekend or two-week escape: **fly south**, **rent a car**, and **surf the west coast** instead of fighting for Channel windswell. Most people fly **CDG or Orly → Faro**; **Lisbon** is the flexible alternative. Road trippers sometimes drive through **Spain** — longer, but doable if you enjoy motorways and stops. Here is the practical side.

## Flights: Paris to Faro

**Transavia**, **easyJet**, and others run **Paris → Faro** in season with block times around **2h 30m–2h 45m**. **Beauvais** low-cost options appear for some dates — factor **bus time** from central Paris when you compare “cheap” fares.

| Hub | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Faro | Short drive to Aljezur (~1.5 h) | Seasonal frequency swings |
| Lisbon | More carriers/year-round | +~3 h driving |

Check **CDG, Orly, and BVA** for your dates; the cheapest airport is not always the cheapest trip once you add transfers.

## Flights: Paris to Lisbon

**Air France**, **TAP**, **Transavia**, **easyJet**, and **Vueling** compete on **Paris → Lisbon**. If Faro is pricey, Lisbon + drive can still win — especially if you want **a night or two in Lisbon** before the coast.

From Lisbon: **A2 south**, then **N120 west** toward Aljezur (~**3 hours** in good traffic).

## Driving through Spain (road trip option)

Some French visitors **drive the whole way** or **fly to Spain** and cross north. Paris → Aljezur is roughly **1,700 km+** depending on route — **16–18 hours** of pure driving split over **two days** is sensible.

**Via Spain (very rough outline):** south-west through France, enter Spain, cross into Portugal on the **A22** corridor near the south — GPS routing beats a blog paragraph. **Tolls** apply in France and Spain; **fatigue** is the real risk — swap drivers and stop overnight.

This only makes sense if you **already wanted a road trip** or are moving **lots of gear**. For most surf holidays, **fly + rent** is simpler.

## Car rental in Portugal

At **Faro** or **Lisbon** airport:

- Reserve **Via Verde** / electronic toll coverage for **A22**.
- **Manual** cars are cheaper if you can drive stick.
- Book **early** for French school holidays — Algarve fills with French and British plates.

Surf logistics: you will drive **daily** between beaches. [Getting to Aljezur](/blog/getting-to-aljezur) + [car rental surf trip Aljezur](/blog/car-rental-aljezur-surf) cover the “why car” argument in detail.

## French travellers: low-cost airports, tolls, and long weekends

**Beauvais and “Paris” fares:** If the ticket says **Paris** but the airport is **Beauvais**, budget **75–90+ minutes** by coach to central Paris — sometimes still a deal, sometimes a false economy once you add **time and transfers**. Compare **CDG and Orly** on the same dates before you commit.

**Driving through France:** Motorway **tolls** stack up on a long run south; péage stops are usually **card-friendly**. If you **road-trip**, plan **two drivers** or an **overnight** — fatigue kills the first surf day.

**Long weekends:** **Ascension** and **early May** see plenty of French plates on the **N120** — book **accommodation** and **car hire** early. ## FAQ

### Paris to Faro flight time?
About **2.5 hours** non-stop on typical scheduled flights.

### CDG or Orly better for Faro?
Depends on the day — compare **total time from your door** including RER/metro or taxi.

For a real surf trip, **yes** — unless you commit to one walkable village and accept missing other beaches. [Complete guide surfing Aljezur](/blog/complete-guide-surfing-aljezur) maps the coastline mindset.
