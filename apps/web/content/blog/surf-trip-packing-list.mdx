## Clothing: summer vs winter on the west coast

**June–September:** T-shirts, shorts, **light jumper** for windy evenings, **rain shell** (yes, it rains sometimes), **flip-flops** + **trainers** for rocky paths.

**April–May & October:** Add **fleece**, **jeans or joggers**, **warmer jacket** for post-surf; you will feel cool in **4/3** wetsuits when the wind blows.

**November–March:** **5/3** territory — pack **beanie**, **warm layers**, **waterproof jacket**, **gloves** if you chill fast (most locals skip surf gloves, but you do you).

**Footwear:** Paths to some beaches are **steep or stony** — something with **grip** beats smooth-soled fashion trainers.

## Sun and skin

The Atlantic sun **reflects off the water** — burns happen fast on a four-hour session.

- **Reef-safe** sunscreen for face (zinc sticks are popular).
- **SPF lip balm**.
- **Sunglasses** + strap for driving the coast road.
- **Hat** for beach hangs.
- **Rash vest** optional under wetsuit in summer for neck protection.

## Documents and money

- **Passport / ID** (post-Brexit UK: passport rules apply — check before travel).
- **Driving licence** + **rental voucher**.
- **Travel insurance** details — **surf cover** if you want real peace of mind.
- **One credit + one debit** — Visa/Mastercard widely accepted; **cash** still handy in tiny village bars.

## First aid and recovery

Keep it proportionate — you are not expedition trekking.

- **Plasters**, **antiseptic**, **ibuprofen** (personal tolerance permitting).
- **Ear drops** if you are prone to swimmer’s ear.
- **Strapping tape** for ribs / feet if you are injury-prone.
- **Lip salve**, **throat lozenges** — **air-con + salt air** dries you out.

## What NOT to bring

- **Huge hard boards** if a **rental** solves your week — unless you are training for something specific.
- **Multiple steamer wetsuits** for a **short trip** — one personal suit + our rental is usually enough laundry pain.
- **Valuables you do not use** — hire cars and beach car parks reward minimalism.
- **Banned or risky items** — check **airline** sports equipment rules before you tape a quiver shut.

## FAQ

### Do I need booties in Aljezur?
**Usually no** in summer; **optional** in winter on rocky entries at some spots. Most people surf **barefoot** in wetsuits year-round here.

### Should I pack a towel for every day?
**Quick-dry microfibre** — one or two rotate fine if you hang them.

### Can I travel carry-on only if I rent gear?
Often **yes** for a week — clothes layers matter more than gadgets.
