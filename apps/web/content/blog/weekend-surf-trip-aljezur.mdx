A weekend is never enough, but Faro-close Aljezur can still deliver two solid dawn sessions plus an afternoon hit on a tight Friday–Sunday trip. You will not cover every break, but you can surf, eat well, and know if you will return longer.

## Can you surf Aljezur on a weekend from Faro?

Yes, if you rent a car at the airport and book accommodation near the beaches. Faro is roughly **1.5 hours** by car; Lisbon is about **3 hours**, which eats too much of a short trip unless you fly in Thursday night.

| Leg | Time (approx.) |
| --- | --- |
| Faro airport → Aljezur | 1h 20–1h 40 |
| Aljezur town → Arrifana | 15–20 min |
| Aljezur town → Monte Clérigo | 10–15 min |

**Tip:** Pre-book the car — weekend queues at Faro in summer are real. Pick an estate or van if you're bringing your own board; otherwise [rent soft-tops from us](/pricing) and skip the airline fees.

## Friday: land, drive, settle, scout

**Evening:** Collect bags, car, and drive west. Check in, unpack, and if there's light left, drive to **Arrifana** or **Monte Clérigo** just to see the sand, wind direction, and who's in the water. No pressure to surf — you're calibrating for Saturday.

Eat somewhere simple — our [restaurant picks](/blog/best-restaurants-aljezur) apply whether you're here three days or three weeks. Get an early night; Saturday is the main day.

## Saturday: main surf day

**Dawn patrol (optional):** If swell and tide align, an early session at [Arrifana](/blog/arrifana-surf-guide) is the classic move — lighter winds, fewer people. Read [how conditions work here](/blog/how-to-read-surf-conditions) so you're not guessing at the car park.

**Late morning / midday:** Second session or rest. If your arms are toast, walk the cliffs or grab coffee in Aljezur town.

**Afternoon:** If the wind turns onshore at your first beach, try **Monte Clérigo** or check **Amoreira** — different exposure, sometimes a cleaner afternoon window.

**Evening:** Dinner in town or near the beach. You're not squeezing in Lagos — keep radius tight so Sunday isn't a slog.

## Sunday: surf, rinse, airport

**Morning:** One more session at the beach that **worked best** yesterday — don't chase novelty on a short trip; repeat what you know.

**Checkout:** Aim to leave the coast by early afternoon for a Faro flight. Build buffer for fuel return and traffic — Sunday evening queues happen.

If you have a late flight, a lazy lunch in Aljezur and a final look at the castle view is a nice bookend.

## What about a three-day weekend?

With Monday off, add **Sunday afternoon surf**, a **slow Monday morning** session at a mellower spot, or a short drive toward **Carrapateira** for a change of scenery (more driving, so only if conditions are average at home beaches).

Compare pacing with our [one-week itinerary](/blog/one-week-aljezur-itinerary) — the weekend version is that plan with everything compressed.

One more reality check: **tide and wind** will matter more on a short trip because you can't "wait for tomorrow" as easily. If Saturday morning is blown out, your whole plan might be Sunday-only in the water — so build **one flex block** (hike, nap, slow lunch) and treat a good Sunday as the win.

### FAQ

### Is one weekend enough to learn to surf?

You can stand up and get hooked, but progression needs volume. Treat the weekend as a **taster**; book lessons locally if you're a complete beginner.

### Which airport is best for a weekend?

**Faro** — shortest drive. Lisbon works only if you accept ~6 hours round-trip driving inside a short trip.

### Should I stay in Aljezur town or at the beach?

For max surf time, **Arrifana or Vale da Telha** cuts driving. Town is better if you want restaurants and market access without staying isolated. See [where to stay](/blog/where-to-stay-aljezur).

### What if the swell is flat?

Hike a section of the Rota Vicentina, explore Aljezur castle, or day-trip toward Sagres (~40 min) to scout other exposures — flat at one beach doesn't always mean flat everywhere.
