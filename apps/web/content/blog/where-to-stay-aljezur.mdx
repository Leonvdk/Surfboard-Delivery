The Aljezur area has four main zones for accommodation. Each has a different vibe, different proximity to the surf, and different trade-offs. Here's what you need to know to pick the right base for your trip.

## Aljezur town

**The practical choice.** Aljezur is the area's only real town — a quiet market settlement built along a valley with a Moorish castle on the hilltop. It has the most facilities: supermarkets, a pharmacy, banks, the Saturday morning market, and the widest choice of restaurants.

**Pros:**
- Most restaurants and shops within walking distance
- Best variety of accommodation (hotels, guesthouses, apartments)
- Saturday market is a highlight (local produce, cheese, honey, bread)
- Centrally located — roughly equidistant from all the surf beaches

**Cons:**
- You'll drive 10-15 minutes to any surf beach
- No beach within walking distance
- Quieter at night (though some would call that a pro)

**Best for:** Couples or groups who want evening dining options and don't mind a short drive to the beach each morning.

## Arrifana

**The surfer's choice.** Arrifana is a tiny cliff-top village perched above the area's best all-round surf beach. The views are spectacular, the vibe is laid-back, and you can see the surf from your morning coffee.

**Pros:**
- Walk to the best surf beach in the area
- Stunning cliff-top setting with Atlantic views
- A few excellent restaurants right in the village
- Strong surf community atmosphere

**Cons:**
- Limited accommodation options (mostly villas and a few guesthouses)
- Fewer shops and restaurants than Aljezur town
- Can feel isolated if you don't drive
- Premium prices during summer

**Best for:** Dedicated surfers and couples who want to wake up, check the waves, and walk down. If surf proximity is your priority, Arrifana is the answer.

## Vale da Telha

**The family choice.** Vale da Telha is a residential area between Aljezur town and Arrifana — a quiet urbanisation of villas, apartments, and holiday homes spread across a pine-covered hillside. It's the most common area for self-catering holiday rentals.

**Pros:**
- Largest selection of holiday rental villas and apartments
- Very quiet and safe — excellent for families with kids
- 5-10 minutes to Arrifana beach by car
- Good value compared to Arrifana village itself
- Several restaurants and a mini-market in the area

**Cons:**
- No walkable beach (you'll drive everywhere)
- Spread-out — not a village centre to stroll around
- Can feel too quiet for solo travellers or nightlife seekers

**Best for:** Families and groups renting a villa. The self-catering setup works perfectly with weekly surf gear rental — we deliver everything to your door, and your villa becomes base camp for the week.

## Monte Clérigo

**The laid-back choice.** Monte Clérigo is a small beach village 6km west of Aljezur. It has a genuinely Portuguese feel — fishing community roots, a handful of restaurants, and one of the area's gentlest surf beaches right on the doorstep.

**Pros:**
- Walk to Monte Clérigo beach (excellent for beginners and families)
- Authentic village atmosphere
- Several good fish restaurants
- Less touristy than Arrifana

**Cons:**
- Very limited accommodation (a few guesthouses and holiday rentals)
- Fewer facilities than Aljezur town
- The beach faces west and can be windblown in summer afternoons
- 15-minute drive to Arrifana and other surf beaches

**Best for:** Families with young children who want a walkable beach and a village atmosphere. Also great for beginners — Monte Clérigo's gentle waves are ideal for first-timers.

## Our recommendation

If it's your first time in the area and you want to surf every day, **Vale da Telha** or **Arrifana** are your best bets. Vale da Telha gives you the most space and value (especially for families), while Arrifana puts you right on top of the waves.

If you're more interested in the total experience — restaurants, markets, exploring — **Aljezur town** is the most complete base.

And if you're after something quiet and authentic with a walkable beach, **Monte Clérigo** delivers.
