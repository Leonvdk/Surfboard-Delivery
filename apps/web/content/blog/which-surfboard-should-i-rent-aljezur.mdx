The single most common wrong decision on an Aljezur surf trip is renting the wrong board. Not because people are careless — because most rental shops let you pick a board by name, and most names mean nothing to someone who hasn't shopped for boards before. "6'6 shortboard" tells you nothing if you don't already know what a 6'6 shortboard feels like under your feet compared to a 7'8 funboard on a shoulder-high summer day at Amado.

This is a guide to picking the right board for **your** situation. It's also a pitch for letting us do it for you, because we have a **calculator** on our homepage that asks you three questions and matches you to the right shape — and that calculator plus a human eye over the result is worth more than any generic "beginners need a longboard" advice on the internet.

## The three inputs that actually matter

**Experience level.** Not what you did on your one lesson in Cornwall three years ago. What you'd honestly do on a shoulder-high day tomorrow at Praia da Arrifana. Rate yourself with the shortest possible descriptor:

- **First-timer** — never surfed, or surfed only in the whitewater. You want maximum stability and paddle power.
- **Improver** — you can catch unbroken waves in small conditions, stand up more often than not, and manage a bottom turn.
- **Intermediate** — you're carving on the wave face, reading which peak to sit at, comfortable up to head-high.
- **Advanced** — you know what board you want. Skip to /surf-gear.

**Weight and height.** A 60 kg surfer needs a very different board from a 90 kg surfer on the same wave. Every board has a volume rating (in litres). Beginners want more litres per kilo; advanced surfers want less. This is why any recommendation that doesn't ask your weight is guessing.

**When you're surfing.** Peak summer at Amado is small, mushy, forgiving. Peak winter at Bordeira is big, powerful, punishing. The same person needs different boards for different weeks. If you're coming in September–October (our shoulder), most intermediates are fine on the same board they'd use in summer. If you're coming in January, we might steer you a shape up.

## The four shapes we rent, matched to level

**8'6 Longboard** — Beginners, absolute first-timers, larger-framed adults learning. Maximum stability, easiest paddling, easiest to catch waves. Slow to turn but that's fine, you're not turning yet. Also a great rental for experienced longboarders who want a stable travel board for cruising Amoreira or Amado on knee-to-waist days. Ideal weight range: any.

**7'8 Funboard** — The workhorse. Improvers and light-framed intermediates. Enough foam under the chest to paddle well, enough length to catch waves easily, enough turn radius that you can start carving. If you can catch and ride broken waves and want to move to the unbroken face, this is probably your board. Ideal weight range: 60–90 kg.

**7'0 Funboard** — Intermediates and heavier-framed improvers. Faster, more responsive, less stable. If you can already turn on a longer board and want to feel the wave face more, step here. Not for total beginners — you'll spend the trip wobbling. Ideal weight range: 65–95 kg.

**6'6 Shortboard (softtop)** — Intermediate-plus surfers only. Fast, tight-turning, requires paddle strength and wave-catching timing. A shortboard on a slow summer day at Amado is a bad time; save it for punchy days at Arrifana or Bordeira. Ideal weight range: 60–85 kg.

## Why we don't publish "the beginner board"

Most rental fleets advertise "the beginner board" — one shape they push at anyone who says "I'm a beginner." That's fine if you're 65 kg, average height, and starting from zero on a small summer day. It fails if you're 95 kg (that board is too small), 55 kg (that board is too big), or already know how to catch broken waves (you'll be bored on day two).

The **fleet-based approach** — you pick, we hand it over — puts the burden of gear knowledge on the customer. The **person-based approach** — you tell us who you are and where you're going, we pick — puts the burden on us. We prefer the second because we do this all day and you're on holiday.
