_Written 2026-07-21. Review quarterly; rewrite each July._

---

## 1. Executive summary

We're in the middle of the highest-intent 8 weeks of the year, and
Leon is away for most of August. The strategic question isn't
"how do we win peak season 2026" — that ship is largely sailed.
It's **how do we use the next 12 months to be so visible for early-
2027 searches that January's holiday-booking traffic finds us
first**.

Three phases, one goal:

| Phase | Months | Job | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Capture** | Jul – Oct 2026 | Book anything still bookable. Collect reviews, first-party data, and photos while we have volume. | Reviews, GBP signals, first-party stats. |
| **Build** | Nov 2026 – Feb 2027 | Publish, index, and cite everything. Nothing published after 30 Nov will rank in time for January. | Content, backlinks, freshness. |
| **Harvest** | Mar – Aug 2027 | Convert early-booker traffic → paid bookings. Fund fleet expansion. | Bookings, revenue, GA. |

The three biggest levers we haven't fully pulled:

1. **Google Business Profile** — every playbook agent flagged this
   as the single biggest local-search lever. Not yet live.
2. **First-party data posts** — 82% AI-citation rate per the deep
   research. Our booking DB is a moat competitors don't have.
3. **Review recency** — matters more than volume in 2026. Need a
   post-rental review flow before Leon leaves for August.

Winter isn't dead. It's search season for next summer's bookings.
Advanced surfers come in winter for the big waves. Gift vouchers
sell in December. Storage and off-season lockup are optional
adjacencies.

## 2. Seasonality & context

### Demand pattern (from Leon's own operating experience)

| Month | Demand for soft-top delivery | What's happening in customer heads |
|---|---|---|
| Jan | Very low | People *researching* summer trips |
| Feb | Very low | Booking window opens for Easter + summer |
| Mar | Low | Easter surf trips being planned |
| Apr | Low-medium | Easter break, some warm-up demand |
| May | Medium | Late-spring trips, family early birds |
| Jun | High | Season warm-up |
| **Jul** | **Very high** | Peak, holidaymakers land |
| **Aug** | **Peak** | Holidaymakers everywhere |
| Sep | High → medium | Kids back at school, quality shoulder |
| Oct | Medium | Advanced surfers, van-lifers |
| Nov | Low | Winter arriving, only diehards |
| Dec | Very low | Christmas market for gift vouchers |

### The search-timing model

Rule of thumb from travel research (and matches Leon's own
Airbnb/hotel booking behaviour): **customers search 4-6 months
before their trip**. Which means:

- Nov 2026 searchers → planning May-Jun 2027 trips.
- Dec 2026 searchers → planning Jun-Jul 2027 trips (peak).
- Jan-Feb 2027 searchers → planning Jul-Aug 2027 trips (peak).

**Google indexes content in ~2-8 weeks depending on freshness
signals. LLMs index continuously but weight <30-day content
heavily.** So anything we want ranked for Jan-Feb 2027 searchers
must be published no later than **late November 2026**.

### Winter isn't dead — three off-season plays

1. **Advanced surfers** — the swell is best in winter. If we had
   even one performance shortboard, we could serve them.
2. **Gift vouchers** — Dec is the second-highest search month for
   "surf lesson gift" and "surf trip gift" in Portugal.
3. **Extended-stay digital nomads** — Aljezur has a growing
   nomad community; monthly board hire is a distinct product.

### Q4 2026 (Oct – Dec): Build the index

**Prime objective**: publish the winter content pipeline; get
everything indexed and cited before the Jan search wave.

- **October**: publish 4 blog posts (topics below). Ship all
  deferred audit items (15-23 from the earlier synthesis).
- **November**: **content freeze at 30 Nov.** After this date,
  no ranking impact for Jan-Feb searchers. Focus on backlink
  building, reviews, and small refresh.

  boards to buy for 2027. Prepare early-booker landing page.

### Q1 2027 (Jan – Mar): Harvest

**Prime objective**: convert incoming Jan-Feb early-booker
traffic; refine offering based on demand signals.

- Track early-booker conversion vs organic sessions.
- Publish "Best time to surf Aljezur 2027" and similar seasonal
  hooks.
- Pre-sell peak-season slots with deposits.

### Q2 2027 (Apr – Jun): Funnel

**Prime objective**: convert peak-season inquiries; prepare
operations.

- Pipeline fleet expansion completed by end of May.
- Publish content aligned with mid-summer search intent.
- Prep the August 2027 automation playbook (assumes Leon travels
  again).

### One-year expected outcome

If we execute this plan cleanly:

- 40-60 Google reviews (up from ~8), review recency continuous
- ~15-20 new blog posts published + 5-8 refreshed
- 3-5 first-party data posts (highest AI-citation-rate content)
- GBP live, 5 star average, weekly photo cadence
- 10+ high-authority citations
- 5-10 quality backlinks from Portugal/travel sites
- **Expected traffic**: 3-5× current organic sessions, weighted
  toward Jan-Aug when the funnel matters most.

1. **Auto-responder on `/contact`**: any booking request in August

## 7. Signaling framework — Google + LLMs

### Continuous signals (every week)

- **GBP**: 1 photo + 1 post = "still alive" signal.
- **Blog**: 1 published or refreshed post = freshness signal.
- **Reviews**: 2+ new = local-pack ranking signal.

### Structured signals (every quarter)

- **Freshness stamp**: bump `dateModified` on canonical posts
  that had real edits.
- **New backlinks**: 2-3 from real relationships.
- **Schema audit**: verify Rich Results still valid.

### Weight signals we already have

- LocalBusiness + SportingGoodsStore schema.
- FAQ + HowTo + Article schema.
- Person schema (Leon).
- BreadcrumbList on deliver-to pages.
- llms.txt for AI answer engines.
- Sitemap with weekly-changing changefreq.

### What we're missing to close

- Review schema on `/reviews` page (deferred audit item 22).
- vatID + taxID in LocalBusiness once NIF supplied.
- `sameAs` array updated with GBP + FB + IG + TripAdvisor URLs.
- WebSite `SearchAction` schema (site search box).
- Speakable schema on FAQ answers (voice search).

## 9. Fleet planning (Q4 2026 → 2027)

Preliminary — refine in December after peak-season data.

Current fleet: **4 soft-top boards** (6'6, 7'0, 7'8, 8'6).

Recommended additions by demand class:
- **Second 8'6 longboard** — highest utilization board, blocks
  double-bookings on peak weeks.
- **Second 7'8 funboard** — same reasoning.
- **Performance shortboard (5'8 or 5'10 hard-top)** — unlocks
  winter/advanced-surfer market. Low volume but high per-day.
- **Kids' 6'0 soft-top** — under-served segment (family group
  bookings).

Budget guide: assume €400-600 per soft-top, €700-1200 for
performance shortboard. Optimistic fleet capex: €2,500-3,500 for
4 boards.

**How to fund**: reserved margin from Sep-Oct bookings
(shoulder-season is high-margin per hour). Gift-voucher revenue
Dec-Feb. Target: €3,000 by end of Feb.
